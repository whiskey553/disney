There’s a new, hot item at Hong Kong Disneyland Resort! And no, it’s not the latest ear headband or a snazzy new hoodie, trash is the talk of the town this season — litter-ally. 

When the all-new, exclusive Push Mystery Figurine set hit the shelves in Hong Kong, there was no time to waste as guests began collecting and trading their finds. Available in stores around Hong Kong Disneyland Resort, each box contains one randomly selected figurine that looks just like the iconic, themed trash cans around the park.

World of Frozen, Toy Story Land, Tomorrowland, “it’s a small world,” Main Street U.S.A and last, but certainly not least, the famous PUSH the Talking Trash Can in Fantasyland are among the six you might pull from a box.

With so many potential styles to choose from, the masterminds behind the collection, Amy and Rachel, shared that they walked the park several times, comparing each and every one before landing on the coveted final six.  

The top priority was to choose something colorful, allowing the themes to be distinguished from one another and catch the eye of potential collectors. From the start, PUSH, the talking trash can who roams around Fantasyland engaging with guests, was a must to include and actually inspired the entire collection to begin with! 

“At the time, we wanted to create something unique to the park, and I immediately thought of making a mini-PUSH because of its popularity,” shared Amy. “We landed in this direction and decided to make a mini trash can mystery set of six.”

And who knew that six miniature figures would create such a phenomenon at the resort! Not only are guests snatching up this exclusive item, they’re even coming to the park in their very own trash-tastic looks. 

Amy and Rachel have been taken aback by the popularity of their creation. In fact, it’s become a special moment in their careers.

“This toy trash can collection has also become a memorable part of my journey. I’m really grateful for the support from my team and my leaders,” shared Rachel. “Everyone contributed their ideas to make it a hit. Seeing a product I helped plan—from the concept and production to its market launch—become so well-loved by everyone brings me great satisfaction.”

We can’t wait to get our hands on these mini figures the next time we’re at Hong Kong Disneyland!

It’s time to grab your jack-o’-lanterns because “Disney Halloween Time” at Hong Kong Disneyland Resort has returned for happy hauntings September 13 through October 31, 2024. There’s no better way to celebrate this bone-chilling season than uncovering experiences that’ll make you scream with excitement!

Visit New Limited-time Disney Hero and Villain Duos  

For the first time ever at Hong Kong Disneyland, a variety of Disney heroes and villains will make appearances in pairs for a limited time this Halloween season. You can find your favorite duos from Walt Disney Animation Studios classics like the Queen and Snow White from “Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs,” Maleficent and Aurora from “Sleeping Beauty” and Jasmine and Jafar from “Aladdin.” Mickey and Friends along with Duffy and Friends will also appear in their fang-tastic Halloween attire throughout Hong Kong Disneyland for some adorable photos as they get into the spirit of the season.

New Halloween Décor and Celebratory Attire

As you make your way into Hong Kong Disneyland, you’ll be welcomed by a BOO-tiful floral Halloween Mickey sporting some pointy fangs and a new signature giant Mickey pumpkin head perfect for a quick pose!

At the Halloween Time Festive Gardens, you can find Mickey and Friends, Winnie the Pooh, Tigger and Eeyore as they strike petrifyingly playful poses in their new adorable Halloween outfits. Duffy and Friends can also be found in new Halloween attire as they greet you at the Duffy and Friends Play House adorned in décor fit for the season.

The Unnerving Return of House of De Vil-lains and “Let’s Get Wicked”

The villainous fashionista, Cruella de Vil, from Disney Animation’s “One Hundred and One Dalmatians,” is once again hosting the House of De Vil-lains with Disney Villains like the Queen, Hades from “Hercules,” the Queen of Hearts from “Alice in Wonderland” and more! Along with these characters, a new cohort of Disney Animation sidekicks are debuting including the Queen’s Raven Bird, Hades’ Pain and Panic, the Queen of Hearts’ Card Soldiers, and Yzma’s Kronk from “The Emperor’s New Groove.” This wickedly fun party invites you to party alongside your favorite Disney Villain as you experience the Halloween bash full of heart-pounding music, villain-inspired projections and artistic villain-themed installations.

The fun doesn’t stop there as fan-favorites such as Maleficent from “Sleeping Beauty,” Ursula from “The Little Mermaid,” Gaston from “Beauty and the Beast” and other iconic Disney Villains perform the award-winning Halloween musical “Let’s Get Wicked,” where they sing and dance under the spotlight.

No Soul Should Miss This Halloween Street Party

Get ready for Halloween music to fill the air as fan-favorites like Mickey Mouse, Minnie Mouse, Goofy and others appear in their adorable outfits on giant jack-o’-lantern whirlies for “Mickey’s Halloween Time Street Party!” If you’re a fan of Duffy and Friends, spot Duffy, ShellieMay, Gelatoni and LinaBell dressed up in Halloween-themed outfits.

This year, the uninvited stepmother from Disney Animation’s “Cinderella,” Lady Tremaine, along with the evil stepsisters, Anastasia and Drizella, will crash Main Street, U.S.A. before the street party to rile up the crowd as they search for their perfect bachelors.

Uncover Devious Delights  

Prepare yourself for terrifyingly terrific treats as Hong Kong Disneyland is serving all kinds of devilish delights! From ghostly goodies like the Crispy Chocolate and Blood Orange Cake at Chart Room Cafe or the Trick or Treat Pumpkin Smoothie from Main Street Corner Cafe Hosted by Coca-Cola, to captivating options like the Pistachio Flavored Soft Serve from Midtown Delights or the Mischievous Platter Combo and the God of Mischief special drink at Starliner Diner, there are plenty of options for all.

What’s this? What’s this? Well, The Pumpkin King, or better known as Jack Skellington from “Tim Burton’s The Nightmare Before Christmas,” has once again crashed the River View Cafe in Hong Kong Disneyland to host Jack Skellington’s The Nightmare Before Dinner where you can enjoy ghoulish goodies such as the Buggy Bew welcome drink and Trick or “Meat” entrée inspired by Halloween Town’s bizarre dining decorum. This creepy-crawly feast will feature Jack, Sally, Oogie Boogie and other inhabitants of Halloween Town as they make a surprise visit during your meal for frightfully fun interactions and photos!

Extra Sweet Treats That’ll Make You Howl

Get your candy bags ready for a classic Halloween tradition and score some tasty sweets at the Trick-or-Treat Trail! As you visit different lands such as Fantasyland, Tomorrowland, Toy Story Land and more, you can discover various candy stations where cast members give out treats that may satisfy even the sweetest of tooths. And if you’re looking to embrace your inner villain or hero, you can find a variety of Halloween apparel, headwear and accessories themed to favorites like Mickey and Friends, Duffy and Friends and The Nightmare Before Christmas that’ll have you looking otherworldly!

Not ready for this ghastly good time to end? Stay tuned to the Disney Parks Blog for all the frightful fun around the world this Halloween season!

Since its opening in 2005, Hong Kong Disneyland Resort has been a home of magic and inspiration for guest and cast members alike. Over 2,000 cast, crew, Imagineers and employees who are at the center of creating happiness and magical guest experiences were recently honored for their decade or more of making magic at Hong Kong Disneyland’s first-ever Service Celebration. An experience that can only happen when you work at Disney, this global tradition made its way to Hong Kong, where honorees and their guests were invited to enjoy themed experiences throughout the park among other food offerings, special character greetings and live performances. 

When they arrived, cast members were greeted by leaders from around the globe and fellow cast members as honorees walked the red-carpet arrival like a Hollywood movie premiere. And did we mention that they were able to enjoy the park without any other guests there? Talk about a dream come true! To top off the evening, attendees gathered together for an exclusive showing of the “Momentous” Nighttime Spectacular, plus a one-night-only custom drone show, “Echoes of Enchantment,” which took cast members through time revisiting Hong Kong Disneyland’s most important milestones. It’s exciting enough to reach a milestone anniversary like working at Disney for over 10 years, let alone celebrating with one big party!

Among the honorees were Rodolf and Randolf, twin brothers who moved from the Philippines to be a part of the Disney magic!  

The pair of twins celebrated their 12 year anniversary of creating happiness and calling Hong Kong their home. “This event reminds us how we truly belong here, and we’re proud to be part of this family!” Randolf shared. 

Let’s dive into what it’s like for them to work together and make magic at one of the happiest places on Earth. 

The brothers began as parade dancers and are now performing side by side in the live stage show, Mickey and The Wondrous Book, at Hong Kong Disneyland Resort. Rodolf and Randolf quickly became a vital part of the resort’s entertainment team, captivating audiences with their enthusiasm and love of performing. “[Their] upbeat, funny and enthusiastic attitude has had a great impact on our team in delivering outstanding shows,” shared Earl, Mickey and The Wondrous Book show captain.  

Their shared passion for performing has made the two inseparable and even led to some funny moments in rehearsal. The two brothers look so identical while dancing, even their colleagues mix them up!  

Despite being once conjoined at the arms, sharing the stage over the years has brought them even closer. “I always have someone to talk to, share everything with, and to support me. [It] gives me more energy,” Rodolf exclaims. “It creates a very strong bond between us,” says Randolf.  

Rodolf and Randolf share a love of spreading joy to the guests who attend their shows. “We really like to perform and it’s great that we can create happiness for guests in this way,” Rodolf shares.

Whether you’re a visitor or Hong Kong Disneyland Resort feels like home, be sure to look out for the brothers doing what they love most (even if you can’t tell them apart!).

This past year, I was able to venture over to Hong Kong Disneyland Resort for the very first time. I can assure you my taste buds enjoyed the trip as much as I did, if not more! As a first-time visitor, I made sure to try all of the can’t-miss foodie experiences at the resort. And you’re in luck, because I’ve put together five of those bites you simply cannot miss when taking a trip to Hong Kong Disneyland Resort, and maybe even an extra one just for good measure. 

Let’s get to it! 

1. Specialty Donuts at Main Street Bakery

Right after entering the park, you’ll be drawn to the sweet smells of the Main Street Bakery. Here you’ll find character-inspired specialty donuts that are as delicious as they are adorable! The talented team expertly puts together these tasty creations resembling Mickey Mouse, Minnie Mouse, Winnie the Pooh, and even Duffy and some of his friends. It’s the perfect way to start your day on a sweet note. 

2. DOLE Whip with Strawberry Slush Float at Jessie’s Snack Roundup

After shrinking down to the size of a toy, it’s only right to treat yourself with a tasty item, and I’ve got the perfect one for you. The DOLE Whip with Strawberry Slush Float from Jessie’s Snack Roundup is absolutely delicious. It perfectly combines the sweetness of the strawberry slush with the pineapple-goodness of the DOLE Whip for a refreshing flavor with every sip. And how cute is that cup inspired by Jessie herself? 

3. Italian Gelato Boat at Small World Ice Cream

Just outside of “it’s a small world,” those with a sweet tooth will be pleased to find this gelato location with some truly incredible creations! As a self-proclaimed gelato aficionado myself, I could not wait to dig into the Italian Gelato. With multiple flavors and mouthwatering toppings served in an “it’s a small world”-themed bowl, I am pleased to share it did not disappoint! 

4. Dim Sum at Crystal Lotus 

What’s better than dim sum? Character dim sum! Crystal Lotus at Hong Kong Disneyland Hotel is an absolute must-try. Whether you’ve been before or it’s your first time there, the dim sum here is not your average offering. The incredibly talented team handmakes all of these detailed creations inspired by some of your favorite characters, like Winnie the Pooh and friends. They’re so cute they’re (almost) too cute to eat. Be sure to take a photo too, because they’re so tasty, you’ll want to eat them all! 

5. Delightful Shakes and Sips from Ink & Plate

Over at Disney’s Hollywood Hotel is the Ink & Plate. This spot is inspired by the magic and imagination of animation! From the décor to the food itself, everything is crafted with tons of creativity and flavor. Strawberry lovers will rejoice at the Colorful Strawberry Shake, while those looking for a colorful and refreshing beverage will enjoy the Animation Pilsner. For a chance to test your artistic skills, see what you can create while you sip with the Color Mixer. All three are delicious and the perfect additions to your meal. 

Bonus Pick! Northern Delights at World of Frozen

Ok, I’ve got one more for you because I couldn’t limit this list to just five. Step into Arendelle at World of Frozen. This Frozen-themed land area opened last year and is filled with delectable bites inspired by this beloved story and its characters. Northern Delights specializes in sweet treats that are truly works of art, such as the Coronation Sundae and Candy Apple Snowball. To see all of the items available to try in World of Frozen, check out our official Foodie Guide here. 

There you have it – some of my can’t miss treats to try at Hong Kong Disneyland Resort. From one foodie to another, I hope you get to try each and every one of them. Happy snacking! 

The foodie adventures to be had are truly limitless at Disney Parks. If you want to stay up to date on anything and everything food and beverage, head on over to our Disney Eats topics page and be sure to follow us @DisneyEats on Instagram and Facebook.

You may not know this, but one of the world’s greatest pianists, Lang Lang, is also one of the world’s biggest Disney fans who has had a lifelong dream of performing in front of a Disney castle. Well, that dream just came true at Hong Kong Disneyland Resort and we’re sharing a look at the historic performance and how the night also became a “dream come true” for some special young artists!

Lang Lang’s one-night-only concert on the stage of the Resort’s Castle of Magical Dreams was inspired by the theme song “Love the Memory” from the Hong Kong Disneyland Nighttime Spectacular “Momentous.”

And during the performance, the world-renowned pianist and string quartet played 14 classic Disney melodies like “Can You Feel the Love Tonight?” from the Walt Disney Animation Studios film “The Lion King,” “Let It Go” from Disney Animation’s “Frozen,” “It’s a Small World,” and many more.

 “Having a live concert in front of a Disney castle has always been a dream of mine,” Lang Lang said. “I’m thrilled that my dream has come true at Hong Kong Disneyland! Disney’s stories and music have played a significant role in my childhood and inspired my musical journey greatly. Through Disney’s unique storytelling, I hope this show will offer the audience an innovative perspective to appreciate classical music.”

Mickey Mouse was the first to join Lang Lang on stage, unveiling the musical journey. Also at the concert, other fan favorite Disney characters made special appearances, including Elsa from the “Frozen” films, Miguel from the Pixar Animation Studios film “Coco”, Belle and Beast from Disney Animation’s “Beauty and the Beast” and many more.

“I was as excited as our guests who enjoyed this one-of-a-kind concert featuring the world-famous piano virtuoso Lang Lang at the Castle of Magical Dreams,” said Michael Moriarty, managing director, Hong Kong Disneyland Resort. “With Lang Lang’s magical interpretation of classic Disney melodies, accompanied by stunning visual effects and sophisticated Disney storytelling, the concert has elevated the already extraordinary entertainment experience at Hong Kong Disneyland to new heights.”

Following the concert, Lang Lang hosted an intimate conversation with a group of aspiring young artists from local schools and community organizations who are inspired by Lang Lang.

Among them were musicians with visual disabilities from the Arts With the Disabled Association Hong Kong, and a wish child from Make-A-Wish Hong Kong named Bells.

Bells is a dedicated fan of the world-renowned pianist. She is currently preparing for her performance diploma examination, and often turns to Lang Lang’s performances for inspiration, studying his skills and techniques to refine her own piano playing. Her admiration for Lang Lang runs deep and meeting him in person is a dream come true for her. Bells was particularly moved by Lang Lang’s rendition of the “Coco” theme song, “Remember Me,” during his performance.

During this exclusive meet-up, she asked Lang Lang for piano-playing tips and showcased the piece she’s preparing for the Make-A-Wish Gala Dinner in March. Inspired by Lang Lang, Bells felt encouraged to remain resilient in the face of challenges, drawing strength from the music and Lang Lang’s own journey.

A beautiful full circle moment as Lang Lang, once a young aspiring musician himself with big Disney dreams, is now inspiring young musicians to follow their musical dreams.

Making today a perfect day every day at Hong Kong Disneyland are the citizens of Arendelle, the cast members now bringing World of Frozen to life for guests. During the grand opening festivities, they gathered for a special #DisneyCastLife tradition: a pep rally and group photo celebrating the inaugural team of cast members selected to open up the gates.

But among the “Frozen” stars and Disney leaders, you might not have noticed some of the familiar-looking faces hidden in plain sight! Thankfully, our frosty friends at the Hong Kong Disneyland Magic Post helped shine a spotlight to ensure they don’t get lost in the woods. Take a look!

They Must Be Royalty

Monica, a carver at Tick Tock Toys & Collectibles, arrived well in advance of the pep rally but never imagined it would lead to being surrounded by Disney luminaries. “I arrived at the Friendship Fountain very early and was hoping to see Queen Anna,” she said. “I didn’t expect that after the honorary guests finished speaking on stage, they would come down and stand on both sides of me.”

Monica ended up front and center in the group photo, between Anna and Elsa, whom she “had always dreamed of seeing. I was so happy and excited! They were so friendly, and talking to them was such a magical moment for me.”

Right behind Monica stood Bob Iger, chief executive officer of The Walt Disney Company, and Josh D’Amaro, chairman of Disney Experiences. All good things, all good things.

Viral Internet Celebrity

A couple rows behind Monica was another very special guest—Mossie, baby troll and love expert in training, who learned more about the human world from cast on hand. “It was very fun that day,” said her nari (or teacher). “Mossie and I made many new friends!”

Looking Cool and Clean

Looking all the way to the back row, did the guy with the casual stretched arm, leaning-against-the-wall pose catch your eye? That’s Michael, who helps keep Arendelle clean with the Custodial team.

“Many citizens here are good friends of mine,” he said. “I saw them busy preparing for Summer Snow Day, so I often come over to help them out, hoping that every nook and corner will be clean and tidy to welcome visitors.” What a guy!

An Ambassador Alumnus

The current and incoming Disney Ambassadors representing Hong Kong Disneyland stand at both ends of the front row, but just behind Gisele and Tony on the far-right is Sammy Phu, Disney Ambassador 2017-18, who is now an attraction operations manager.

“I was very glad to witness the moment,” said Sammy, who was excited to also meet the incoming Global Disney Ambassador 2024-25 team at the grand opening, alongside his daughter, Louise (next to him on the left), a fellow cast member in Human Resources.

As a Manor of Fact …

Finally, you might have spotted a citizen of Arendelle who participated in another #DisneyCastLife moment this past summer. Toward the front-right is Annto, who represented Mystic Manor at our global gathering of Haunted Mansion cast representatives across Disney Parks for the world premiere of “Haunted Mansion” at Disneyland Resort.

“I am incredibly grateful that the celebration brought together some old and new friends,” said Annto, who enjoys traveling and making friends in new places. Consider it no surprise she and Taeny, her fellow “Haunted Mansion” premiere visitor, are now citizens of Arendelle—but don’t worry, both still visit Lord Henry and Albert back in Mystic Point, too.
